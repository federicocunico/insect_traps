# Peer Review: "Multi-Camera Acquisition and Smartphone-Based Diagnostics for Automated Detection of *Scaphoideus titanus*"

## Summary

The paper introduces a genuinely valuable resource: a controlled high-resolution ("Hi-Res") multi-camera acquisition box and a paired smartphone ("Low-Res") dataset of *S. titanus* on yellow sticky traps, imaged from the *same* physical traps, plus a benchmark against the Checola et al. Literature dataset across five experiments. The hardware/acquisition contribution and the same-trap paired Hi-Res/Low-Res design are real strengths and well-motivated. However, in its current form the manuscript is not publishable: the abstract/intro/conclusion describe a "YOLO-based pipeline" that does not match the multi-architecture benchmark actually run; the central narrative framing (title promises "Smartphone-Based Diagnostics") is not delivered; several quantitative claims are over-stated with hyperbolic language; the experimental section contains a serious cross-architecture metric-comparability flaw and an unaddressed data-leakage issue in the "combined" dataset; and the manuscript is littered with placeholders (`\todo`, `\fc`, `\textcolor{red}`, `XXX` instance counts, a conclusion marked "TO BE COMPLETELY REVISED"). The science underneath is sound enough to support a strong paper after major revision, but the framing, metric protocol, and presentation all need substantial work.

### Issue 2: Abstract, introduction, and conclusion claim a "YOLO-based pipeline" but the paper benchmarks multiple architecture families
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:** `00_abstract.tex` sentence 3 and contribution list (sentence 4); `01_introduction.tex` lines 59--61 (third contribution); `06_conclusions.tex` line 6; vs. `04_exps.tex` EXP5 (lines 29--31) and `05_results.tex` EXP5 (Table `tab:exp5`).
**Problem:** The abstract and intro frame the methodological contribution as "a YOLO-based detection system" / "a YOLO-based detection pipeline," and the conclusion says "we benchmarked several state-of-the-art YOLO architectures." But the actual study benchmarks six YOLO variants *plus* Faster R-CNN (ResNet-50) and RT-DETR-L, and uses the cross-architecture comparison as a stated validation goal (EXP5 rationale). The framing under-sells and mischaracterizes the work: it is a multi-architecture benchmark, not a YOLO pipeline. This is a fundamental consistency failure between the claimed and the delivered contribution.
**Fix:** Reframe the contribution throughout as a benchmark of single-stage (YOLO), two-stage (Faster R-CNN), and transformer (RT-DETR) detectors. Update the abstract contribution list, the third intro contribution, and the conclusion accordingly. If the authors genuinely want a "YOLO pipeline" as the deliverable, then EXP5 should be reframed as a control experiment, not a co-equal benchmark — but the current text does both inconsistently.

### Issue 3: Title promises "Smartphone-Based Diagnostics" but the paper delivers no diagnostic/decision story; the smartphone dataset is barely represented in the abstract
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:** `main.tex` line 188 (title); `00_abstract.tex` (entire — Low-Res/smartphone is never mentioned); `05_results.tex` (results emphasize Hi-Res superiority).
**Problem:** (a) The title's word "Diagnostics" implies a diagnostic capability — species-level decision-making, a usable mobile tool, an operational decision threshold, or at minimum a deployment evaluation. The paper provides none of these; it provides bounding-box detection mAP. There is no diagnostic pipeline, no field-deployed app, no decision/operating-point analysis tied to monitoring outcomes. (b) The abstract never mentions the Low-Res/smartphone dataset at all, even though it is half the data contribution and is named in the title. The title and abstract are therefore mutually inconsistent and both over-claim relative to the evidence.
**Fix:** Either (i) soften the title (e.g., "...Multi-Camera and Smartphone Image Acquisition for Automated Detection of *S. titanus* on Yellow Sticky Traps"), dropping "Diagnostics," or (ii) add a genuine smartphone-deployment/diagnostic analysis (operating-point selection for a monitoring decision, count accuracy per trap, latency on-device). Regardless, rewrite the abstract to explicitly describe the Low-Res smartphone dataset and the controlled-vs-uncontrolled comparison, since this is the framing the title leads with.

### Issue 4: EXP5 description in Experiments and Results contradicts the actual EXP5 protocol (3-fold @640 vs 5-fold full-resolution sweep)
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` lines 29--31 ("3-fold cross-validation at a standard 640-pixel resolution"); `05_results.tex` EXP5 caption (Table `tab:exp5` says "3-fold CV") and text lines 151--164; vs. `experiment_results.txt` EXP5 block (lines 124--151), which is 5-fold CV over 512/640/768/1024.
**Problem:** The Experiments section, the results paragraph, and the Table 5 caption all describe EXP5 as 3-fold CV at a single 640 px resolution with only two models. The actual EXP5 (per `experiment_results.txt`) is a 5-fold CV full-resolution sweep (512/640/768/1024) for both Faster R-CNN and RT-DETR. The reported numbers in Table 5 (e.g., RT-DETR-L 0.9117 mAP@50 on Hi-Res, Faster R-CNN 0.7370 mAP@50-95) do not appear anywhere in the updated results file. This is a protocol/description mismatch, not merely stale numbers, and it changes the experiment's scientific claim (a single-resolution spot-check vs. a resolution sweep).
**Fix:** Rewrite the EXP5 description in both `04_exps.tex` and `05_results.tex` to state 5-fold CV across 512/640/768/1024 for Faster R-CNN and RT-DETR, and regenerate Table 5 from `experiment_results.txt`. This also lets EXP5 be compared directly against EXP2's resolution sweep, strengthening the cross-architecture resolution conclusion.

### Issue 5: Cross-architecture P/R/F1 are not comparable — different confidence operating points (best-F1 vs 0.5 score threshold)
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` Evaluation Metrics (lines 34--46); `05_results.tex` EXP5 (lines 151--164); `experiment_results.txt` EXP5 Faster R-CNN rows (e.g., Low-Res 512: P=0.5442, R=0.8213) vs RT-DETR rows.
**Problem:** YOLO Precision/Recall/F1 in this framework are reported at the best-F1 confidence operating point, whereas Faster R-CNN P/R appear to be computed at a fixed 0.5 score threshold (the Faster R-CNN rows show a systematic high-recall/low-precision signature — e.g., recall ~0.82--0.92 with precision as low as 0.54 — characteristic of a fixed low score threshold rather than an F1-optimal one). Comparing P/R/F1 across detectors selected at different operating points is methodologically invalid and will mislead any reader who compares the precision/recall columns across Table 1/2 (YOLO) and Table 5 (Faster R-CNN/RT-DETR). mAP is operating-point-independent and unaffected, but P/R/F1 are not.
**Fix:** Recompute all detectors' P/R/F1 at the *same* operating-point rule (best-F1 confidence per fold, or a single fixed threshold applied to all detectors), and state the rule explicitly in the Evaluation Metrics subsection. If a uniform operating point cannot be obtained for Faster R-CNN, drop its P/R/F1 columns and compare only on mAP, with an explicit caveat that P/R are not operating-point-aligned.

### Issue 6: "Combined" dataset leaks the Literature test domain into training; the clean cross-test must use Hi-Res+Low-Res, and this distinction is never made explicit
**Severity:** Critical
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` EXP4 (lines 25--27, "a union of all three datasets"); `05_results.tex` EXP4 (lines 125--127) and EXP3 (lines 93--95); `experiment_results.txt` EXP3 rows for `Hi-Res + Low-Res → Literature` (lines 91--92) vs `Literature → Hi-Res` rows.
**Problem:** "All Combined" = Hi-Res + Low-Res + Literature. Any model trained on "All Combined" has seen the Literature domain during training, so it cannot be used to claim generalization *to* Literature — that would be evaluating on a domain represented in training (leakage). The updated results (`experiment_results.txt`) correctly add leakage-free `Hi-Res+Low-Res → Literature` cross pairs precisely to avoid this, but the manuscript's EXP3 table (`tab:exp3`) omits those rows entirely and the EXP4 discussion treats "All Combined" generalization without flagging that its apparent cross-domain robustness is partly memorization of the held-in Literature domain. The paper must make the leakage distinction explicit.
**Fix:** (1) In EXP3, report the leakage-free `Hi-Res+Low-Res → Literature` (and `Literature → Hi-Res+Low-Res`) pairs that now exist in `experiment_results.txt`, since these are the only valid cross-domain-to-Literature tests. (2) In EXP4, explicitly state that "All Combined" includes the Literature domain and therefore its evaluation is in-domain, not a generalization claim; contrast it with "Hi-Res+Low-Res" which is the clean held-out configuration relative to Literature. (3) Add a sentence in the Evaluation Metrics or EXP4 text defining the leakage and why two combination variants are reported.

### Issue 7: Hyperbolic, unscientific language ("vividly," "catastrophic," "dramatic," "unusable," "mandating")
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `05_results.tex` EXP3 paragraph (lines 93--95): "Table 3 vividly demonstrates," "catastrophic performance drops," "The inverse is even more dramatic," "an unusable 0.0848," "mandating generalized training solutions."
**Problem:** This is editorializing, not scientific reporting. "Vividly," "catastrophic," "dramatic," and "unusable" are subjective intensifiers inappropriate for a methods paper, and "mandating" over-claims a prescriptive conclusion from a single benchmark. The domain gap is real and worth reporting, but the prose asserts more than the table shows (e.g., 0.0848 mAP being "unusable" is an interpretation, not a measured fact).
**Fix:** Rewrite in neutral, quantitative language: e.g., "Cross-domain transfer degrades sharply: a Hi-Res-trained YOLO11s reaches 0.40 mAP@50 on Literature and 0.085 when the direction is reversed, indicating strong domain specificity." Remove all intensifiers and the prescriptive "mandating."

### Issue 8: "Minimum optimal/viable resolution" is promised but never identified
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` EXP2 rationale (lines 17--19, "identifying the minimum optimal resolution for reliable detection"); `05_results.tex` Impact of Image Resolution (lines 49--51).
**Problem:** EXP2's stated purpose is to identify the minimum optimal/viable resolution, and the Related Works section even cites Laekeman et al. for a μm/pixel threshold (`02_related_v2.tex` line 15). But the results paragraph never identifies any threshold or "minimum optimal resolution"; it only observes that performance increases monotonically up to 1024 px. No knee-point analysis, no μm/pixel mapping tying the box's measured 26.6 μm/pixel sampling to the resolution sweep, and no stated minimum. The contribution promised is undelivered.
**Fix:** Either deliver the analysis — identify a knee/diminishing-returns point, and connect the input-resolution sweep to effective μm/pixel on the insect (the method already reports 26.6 μm/pixel and insect body length 4.8--5.8 mm, so an effective-pixels-on-target figure is computable) — or remove the "minimum optimal resolution" claim from the EXP2 rationale and the abstract-level framing.

### Issue 9: Single-class detection — meaning and use of mAP, and the species-discrimination claim, are not reconciled
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `03_method.tex` Annotation Procedure (lines 135--137, "assigned to the target class *S. titanus*"); `04_exps.tex` Evaluation Metrics; throughout intro/abstract claims about "discriminating *S. titanus* from visually similar taxa."
**Problem:** The dataset is annotated with a *single* class (*S. titanus*). With one class, AP/mAP measure detection+localization only; they do *not* measure species discrimination, because there is no competing class to confuse with. Yet the abstract, intro (lines 39--45), and method (line 24) repeatedly motivate high resolution by the need to *discriminate S. titanus from visually similar taxa*. The experiments cannot support a discrimination claim: visually similar non-target insects are (at best) implicit background, and the metric never tests "is this *S. titanus* vs *Orientus ishidae*?". The Checola dataset is explicitly 2-class (`Checola2024Dataset` in the bib), so the comparison may also conflate a 2-class problem reduced to 1-class. This is a soundness gap between the stated motivation and what is measured.
**Fix:** (1) State clearly that the task is single-class detection and that mAP measures detection/localization, not inter-species discrimination. (2) If the Literature dataset is 2-class, describe exactly how it was reduced to a single *S. titanus* class and whether *O. ishidae* annotations became background or were discarded (this materially affects the cross-dataset numbers). (3) Temper the "discrimination from visually similar taxa" framing, or add an experiment with non-target hard negatives / a second class to actually substantiate it.

### Issue 11: "No traps from the same acquisition batch in train and test" claim is asserted but not substantiated, and is in tension with the same-trap Hi-Res/Low-Res design
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` Evaluation Metrics (lines 44--45, "ensuring that no traps from the same acquisition batch were present in both training and testing sets"); `03_method.tex` lines 103, 131 (every trap imaged in both Hi-Res and Low-Res; Low-Res has full/half/quadrant crops of the same trap).
**Problem:** (a) The fold-construction protocol is never described: are splits image-level or trap-level? This matters enormously because the Low-Res set contains multiple views (full-trap, two half-trap, four quadrant images) of the *same* physical trap (`03_method.tex` line 93). An image-level random split would place different crops of the same trap in both train and test, leaking ground-truth specimens and inflating intra-dataset mAP (Tables 1, 2, 4, 5). (b) In the combined experiments, the same trap exists in both Hi-Res and Low-Res; without trap-level grouping across modalities, a trap's Hi-Res image could train while its Low-Res image tests. The "no same batch in train/test" sentence is too vague to verify and conflicts with the stated multi-view, same-trap design unless grouping is explicitly trap-ID-based across modalities.
**Fix:** Describe the exact fold construction: confirm splits are grouped by physical trap ID (or by some larger unit), applied consistently across Hi-Res/Low-Res/quadrant variants and across modalities in the combined experiments. If splits are image-level, this is a leakage bug that must be fixed and the affected tables re-run. State the grouping unit and number of traps per fold explicitly.

### Issue 12: Reproducibility — training hyperparameters, epochs, optimizer, pretraining, and augmentation specifics are missing
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `03_method.tex` Data Preprocessing (lines 148--152, augmentations "in some experiments" — which?); commented-out YOLO pipeline subsection (lines 155--161); `04_exps.tex` Implementation Details (lines 48--52).

The manuscript also claims the pipeline is "fully reproducible" (line 52) while omitting essentially all hyperparameters and providing no code/data availability statement.
**Fix:** Add a complete training-configuration table (per architecture): epochs, batch size, optimizer + LR schedule, image size, pretraining source, augmentation set and whether it was on/off per experiment, NMS threshold, confidence-selection rule, seeds. Add an explicit Data Availability statement (the box dataset and the smartphone dataset — where will they be released?) and a code-availability statement. Reconcile the "fully reproducible" claim with what is actually provided.

### Issue 13: Statistical significance is never tested despite many near-tied comparisons
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `05_results.tex` EXP1 (lines 11--13, "consistently outperformed"; bolded "best" per dataset); EXP4 (lines 125--127); EXP5 (lines 149--151).
**Problem:** Many headline comparisons are within one standard deviation of each other. E.g., in Table 1 on Hi-Res, YOLOv5s 0.9432±0.0062 vs YOLO11s 0.9422±0.0105 vs YOLOv8s 0.9417±0.0047 are statistically indistinguishable, yet the text and bolding imply a "best" model. The claim that Hi-Res "consistently outperformed" Literature/Low-Res on mAP@50 is modest (0.94 vs 0.91--0.92) and the cross-dataset/combined deltas are sometimes within noise. No significance testing (paired tests across folds, confidence intervals) is reported anywhere.
**Fix:** Add paired statistical tests across folds for the key comparisons (Hi-Res vs others; combination vs single-domain; architecture comparisons), or at minimum stop declaring "best"/"consistently outperformed" where differences are within the reported std. Tone down "consistently outperformed" — the mAP@50 gap is small; the real story is mAP@50-95 (localization), which should be stated as such.

### Issue 14: mAP@50-95 vs "mAP@0.5 and recall" — the conclusion claims a recall benefit that the headline metric does not isolate, and metrics list mentions thresholds not consistently reported
**Severity:** Major
**Location:** `04_exps.tex` Evaluation Metrics (lines 38--43: lists Precision/Recall/F1, AP@0.5, mAP@0.5--0.95 — no mAP@75); `06_conclusions.tex` line 6 ("particularly in terms of mAP@0.5 and recall"); `05_results.tex` (recall is not discussed in the results text at all).
**Problem:** (a) The conclusion attributes the Hi-Res benefit "particularly in terms of mAP@0.5 and recall," but the results section never analyzes recall, and the actual data (`experiment_results.txt`) show the Hi-Res advantage is dominated by mAP@50-95 (localization at strict IoU: ~0.79 vs ~0.58--0.61), not by mAP@50 (which is close across datasets) and not clearly by recall (Hi-Res recall ~0.88 vs Low-Res ~0.86 — a small gap). The conclusion's metric attribution is inconsistent with the evidence. (b) The metric definitions list "AP at IoU=0.5" separately from mAP@50, which for a single-class detector are the same quantity — redundant/confusing. No mAP@75 is defined or reported, so remove any implication of it.
**Fix:** Make the conclusion's metric claim match the data: the dominant Hi-Res advantage is at strict IoU (mAP@50-95 / localization precision), with mAP@50 differences small. Clarify in the metrics list that for single-class detection AP@0.5 = mAP@50. Remove unreported thresholds.

### Issue 16: Related Works cites a μm/pixel "minimum viable resolution" threshold (Laekeman) that the paper sets up but never uses, leaving a dangling promise

### Issue 19: "JPEG" / "minimally compressed" vs "minimizes artifacts" tension, and 8-bit sRGB limits the fine-morphology claim
**Severity:** Minor
**Location:** `03_method.tex` lines 18 ("saved as JPEG files with 8-bit depth"), 87 ("minimally compressed JPEG"); intro/abstract claims of "superior pixel density" and capturing "subtle coloration patterns."
**Problem:** The paper repeatedly stresses fidelity for subtle coloration and fine morphology, but acquires 8-bit JPEG. JPEG is lossy (chroma subsampling, block artifacts) and 8-bit limits coloration-gradient fidelity — somewhat in tension with the "minimizes artifacts" / "subtle coloration patterns" claims. This does not invalidate the work but should be acknowledged.
**Fix:** Acknowledge the JPEG/8-bit choice and its trade-offs, and (if true) justify it as adequate for the detection task, or report whether raw/lossless capture was considered. Avoid over-claiming "subtle coloration" fidelity from a single-class detection benchmark that never measures color.
